A Tale of Two Learners

"You always pass failure on the way to success." -- Mickey Rooney

The Flex Learner

Once upon a time there was a learner who wanted to become a web developer. She was interested in attending class full-time but couldn't quit her job. It wasn't financially feasible but she didn't want to give up on her dream. The learner was very sad until one day a flex program opened at the school she most wanted to attend. She would work during the day, attend class part-time, and squeeze homework in between. She knew it would be tough but that didn't deter her from her goal to be a kickass developer. She was stoked beyond words. Thus began the journey of one coder.

The Full-Time Learner

Once upona time there was a learner who wanted to become a web developer. She was interested in attending class full-time and was able to do so. She chose the full stack program at the school she most wanted to attend. She knew it would be tough but she was glad she would get to delve in full force. Without work to distract her, she could focus only on the code. She would attend class each day and do homework for several hours in the evenings and on weekends. Her life would be all about the code. She was stoked beyond words. Thus began the journey of one coder.

About Me

If there is no stuggle, there is no progress. --Frederick Douglas

  • 2001-2010

    My Humble Beginnings

    I graduted from Agnes Scott College and entered the world of education! With B.A. in Spanish and a minor in education, I was excited and open to the possibilities.

  • 2011-2014

    Reform and Challenges

    I entered the classroom bright-eyed and full of ideas. Using my creativity, I developed lessons and a curriculum that reflected my students and where I worked. Being a teacher and working with curriculum came naturally. Education began to change into something I did not recognize. Tests became more important and teaching students to think became less of a priority. It was a numbers game and I wasn't intersted in playing. I started considering my other options.

  • 2015 - 2016

    Then there was code!

    I kept my day job but code became a majoy part of my life. In my free time, I started Free Code Camp and also dabbled in other free resources. However, I found when I got stuck, it was sometimes difficult to find the answer. My Google game was pretty good but I needed some additional support.

  • 2017

    Enter DigitalCrafts

    I applied and toured several schools but I really liked DigitalCrafts. Evenually, I joined the DigitalCrafts flex program and I haven't looked back. I still work and I code whenever possible. So far, it has been enlightening, challenging, thrilling and frustrating. Now all I do is...
